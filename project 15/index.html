WHAT IOT IS ?

The **Internet of Things (IoT)** refers to a network of physical devices embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies to connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet. Here are some key points: - **Devices**: Includes everything from smart home devices like thermostats and security systems to industrial machinery and wearable technology. - **Applications**: Used in various fields such as healthcare, agriculture, transportation, and manufacturing to improve efficiency and gather data. - **Benefits**: Enhances automation, provides real-time monitoring, and enables data-driven decision-making. - **Challenges**: Security and privacy concerns, as well as regulatory and standardization issues.

the devices in IOT

IoT devices can be broadly categorized into **general devices** and **sensing devices**: 1. **General IoT Devices**: These include smart home devices like smart speakers, smart TVs, and connected appliances. They focus on enhancing convenience and automation in daily tasks. 2. **Sensing IoT Devices**: These devices are equipped with sensors to collect data from the environment. Common types include: - **Temperature Sensors**: Measure ambient temperature. - **Motion Sensors**: Detect movement and are used in security systems. - **Air Quality Sensors**: Monitor pollution levels and air quality. - **Proximity Sensors**: Detect the presence of objects or people.
